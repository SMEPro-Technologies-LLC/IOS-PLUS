REGULATION (EU) 2024/1689 · HIGH-RISK AI SYSTEMS

EU AI Act compliance matrix. Article by article. Architecturally aligned.

The IOS+ platform was built before the EU AI Act took final form, but the architectural commitments converge with what the Act requires of high-risk AI systems. This matrix documents that convergence — article by article, with the specific IOS+ subsystem that addresses each requirement.

Self-attested architectural alignment. This matrix documents how the IOS+ platform's architecture addresses each EU AI Act requirement as of May 2026. Formal conformity assessment per Article 43 is independent of this documentation. Final regulatory determination is the responsibility of the deploying institution and the relevant notified body.

Art. 9
Risk Management System
IOS+ · Compliance OS
Aligned

Regulatory Requirement

Article 9 requires a documented, continuous risk management process across the full AI lifecycle — identifying and evaluating known and foreseeable risks to health, safety, and fundamental rights. The process must be iterative and updated as new risks emerge.

IOS+ Architectural Alignment

The Gate 530 dimensional matrix operates as a continuous, runtime risk assessment system. Every AI request is evaluated against six compliance dimensions before execution, producing a deterministic risk verdict (PASS, BLOCK, or ESCALATE). Risks are not assessed at design time alone — they are assessed on every inference.

  • Six-dimensional risk evaluation per request
  • Tenant-scoped risk thresholds (configurable)
  • Escalation ladder (Claim 8) for risk pattern detection
  • Quarantine queue for novel risk scenarios requiring human review

Art. 10
Data and Data Governance
UDM · Compliance OS
Aligned

Regulatory Requirement

Article 10 mandates data governance practices ensuring training, validation, and testing datasets are relevant, representative, and free from errors and biases. Data governance must address data collection, preparation, and any required annotation, labeling, and cleaning.

IOS+ Architectural Alignment

The Universal Decoding Matrix is the data governance layer for inputs entering the AI pipeline. Every data element is mapped through the Five-Layer Ontological Crosswalk before being made available to the model — ensuring semantic consistency, regulatory classification, and provenance tracking from origin to inference.

  • Semantic translation across CIP, SOC, NAICS, SIC, ISIC, HS-HTS
  • Schema reconciliation across portal and ERP sources
  • L3 Resolver binds each data element to applicable regulatory regime
  • Data lineage preserved in evidence packages at L4
UDM L3 Resolver Ontological Crosswalk Data Lineage
Art. 11
Technical Documentation
IOS+ · Evidence Ledger
Aligned

Regulatory Requirement

Article 11 requires technical documentation drawn up before the AI system is placed on the market or put into service, demonstrating compliance with Articles 8 to 15. Documentation must be kept up to date and provide national competent authorities with sufficient information to assess compliance.

IOS+ Architectural Alignment

The IOS+ platform is documented across a multi-volume engineering body — Architecture, Cryptographic Audit Fabric, Schema and Role Architecture, Dimension Matrix, RAG Pipeline, Deployment Runbook. Each document is version-controlled and updated with each platform release. The Evidence Ledger provides authoritative cryptographic record of every architectural decision propagated to production.

  • Vol I — IOS+ Architecture (L1–L7 pipeline reference)
  • Vol II — Gate 530 Compliance Framework
  • Vol III — Live Deployment and Operational Integrity
  • Engineering Body — Internal architecture spec (NDA-required)

Art. 12
Record-Keeping
Evidence Ledger · IOS+
Exceeds

Regulatory Requirement

Article 12 requires automatic logging of events while the high-risk AI system is operating. Logs must enable identification of situations that may result in risks to fundamental rights or substantial modifications, and facilitate post-market monitoring.

IOS+ Architectural Alignment — Exceeds

The Evidence Ledger goes beyond Article 12's logging requirement. Every event is Ed25519-signed over JCS-canonical records per RFC 8785 — meaning logs are not only retained, they are cryptographically authenticated and independently verifiable by any qualified third party without trust in SMEPro or the operating institution.

  • Ed25519 cryptographic signatures (RFC 8032)
  • JCS canonical form for verification consistency (RFC 8785)
  • SHA-256 request and response hashing (FIPS 180-4)
  • Two-layer WORM enforcement at database tier (RBAC + row-level trigger)
  • Independent verifiability by any third party with public key
Ed25519 JCS / RFC 8785 WORM Database Independent Verification
Art. 13
Transparency and Provision of Information to Deployers
IOS+ · Compliance OS
Aligned

Regulatory Requirement

Article 13 requires high-risk AI systems to be sufficiently transparent for deployers to interpret outputs and use them appropriately. Information must include intended purpose, performance characteristics, expected lifetime, and instructions for use.

IOS+ Architectural Alignment

Every IOS+ decision exposes the reasoning behind it. Gate 530 evaluations produce per-dimension scores and textual rationales — surfaced to deployers via the inference response API. Every quarantined request includes the specific UCO node and dimension that triggered the action, so deployers and reviewers understand why an outcome occurred.

  • Per-dimension scoring exposed in API responses
  • Textual rationale per evaluated UCO node
  • Administrative dashboard for compliance queue review
  • Documented intended purpose, capabilities, and limitations per deployment
Gate 530 Rationale API Transparency Admin Dashboard
Art. 14
Human Oversight
Compliance OS · IOS+
Exceeds

Regulatory Requirement

Article 14 requires high-risk AI systems to be designed and developed to allow effective oversight by natural persons during their use. Human oversight must enable the deployer to fully understand capabilities and limitations, monitor outputs, and intervene or interrupt operation when necessary.

IOS+ Architectural Alignment — Exceeds

Human oversight is not a feature in IOS+ — it is a first-class architectural primitive. The ESCALATE policy action and the administrative quarantine queue exist specifically to route decisions to human reviewers. The POST /v1/compliance/queue/:id/clear and POST /v1/compliance/queue/:id/block endpoints are the formal mechanisms by which human decisions resume or halt AI execution.

  • ESCALATE policy action routes requests to human reviewers
  • Quarantine queue parks execution context for review
  • Administrative API endpoints for clear/block decisions
  • Execution context preserved during suspension, resumable after review
  • Escalation Ladder (Claim 8) prevents review queue flooding

Art. 15
Accuracy, Robustness and Cybersecurity
Compliance OS · Evidence Ledger
Aligned

Regulatory Requirement

Article 15 requires high-risk AI systems to maintain appropriate levels of accuracy, robustness, and cybersecurity throughout their lifecycle, and to resist errors, faults, inconsistencies, and adversarial attacks (including data poisoning, model evasion, and confidentiality attacks).

IOS+ Architectural Alignment

Gate 530's deterministic dimensional evaluation produces reproducible outputs — same input, same decision, every time. Reproducibility is the foundation of accuracy and robustness claims. Cybersecurity is addressed through the isolated sidecar architecture, role-based database access, and append-only evidence ledger that resists tampering even from privileged adversaries.

  • Deterministic gate evaluation (3–5ms at P99)
  • Isolated sidecar boundary (Unix domain socket)
  • Cryptographic integrity for every evaluated decision
  • Two-layer WORM enforcement prevents log tampering
  • Bifurcated key custody (on-prem soft HSM / Cloud KMS) for tenant-specific threat models
Deterministic Gate Sidecar Isolation WORM Audit Bifurcated Custody
Art. 16
Obligations of Providers of High-Risk AI Systems
IOS+ Platform Operations
Aligned

Regulatory Requirement

Article 16 establishes the umbrella obligations of providers — ensuring high-risk systems comply with Articles 8 to 15, indicating provider identity on the system, having a quality management system in place, ensuring conformity assessment is conducted, registering the system, and ensuring the system bears CE marking before placing on the EU market.

IOS+ Architectural Alignment

SMEPro's role as platform provider includes operational responsibility for the IOS+ infrastructure itself. The integrated subsystems — IOS+, COS, UDM, Evidence Ledger — together satisfy the technical requirements of Articles 9–15. The Trust Center documents provider identity, conformity posture, and architectural commitments. Conformity assessment for specific deployments is performed jointly with the deploying institution and the relevant notified body.

  • Provider identity and platform branding embedded in every signed evidence record
  • Trust Center documenting compliance posture
  • Quality management practices (versioned engineering body, CI/CD)
  • Notified-body engagement protocol (deployment-specific)

Art. 17
Quality Management System
IOS+ Platform Operations
Partial

Regulatory Requirement

Article 17 requires providers to put in place a documented quality management system covering regulatory compliance strategy, design control, technical specifications, data management, risk management, post-market monitoring, incident reporting, and resource management.

IOS+ Architectural Alignment — Partial

Engineering and architectural quality controls are in place: versioned engineering body, deterministic gating with reproducibility, append-only audit, comprehensive test infrastructure. Formal ISO 9001 / ISO/IEC 42001 certification is on the roadmap but not yet completed. Documentation of the formal quality management system is maturing alongside platform commercial readiness.

  • Versioned engineering body (architectural and operational documentation)
  • CI/CD with automated testing and integration verification
  • Deterministic and reproducible execution paths
  • Post-market monitoring via signed evidence telemetry
  • In development: ISO 9001 / ISO/IEC 42001 conformance documentation

Source authority and scope of this matrix.

This matrix references Regulation (EU) 2024/1689 of the European Parliament and of the Council establishing harmonised rules on artificial intelligence (the EU AI Act). The full text of the Regulation is available via the official EUR-Lex repository and is mirrored at artificialintelligenceact.eu, the public reference maintained by the Future of Life Institute.

This matrix addresses Articles 9 through 17 — the core technical and operational requirements applicable to providers of high-risk AI systems under Sections 2 and 3 of the Regulation. It does not address obligations specific to deployers (Article 26), general-purpose AI providers (Articles 51–55), or AI systems falling outside the high-risk classification.

This matrix does not constitute legal advice or formal conformity assessment. Conformity assessment per Article 43 must be conducted by the deploying institution and, where applicable, the relevant notified body designated under Article 31. The architectural commitments documented here are SMEPro's representations of platform capabilities as of May 2026 and are subject to ongoing engineering evolution.
